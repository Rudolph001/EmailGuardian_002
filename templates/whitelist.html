Whitelist Information
Domain Whitelist
  • Whitelisted domains automatically trust all emails from that domain
  • Both sender and recipient domains are checked
  • Domain matching is case-insensitive
  • Subdomains are NOT automatically included
Example: Adding trusted.com will whitelist all emails from *@trusted.com
Email Whitelist
  • Individual email addresses that are always trusted
  • Exact email address matching
  • Takes precedence over domain rules
  • Email matching is case-insensitive
Note: Whitelisted items bypass most security rules. Use carefully and review regularly.
Security Considerations
Best Practices
  • Regularly review whitelist entries
  • Use specific emails over broad domains
  • Document business justification
  • Monitor whitelisted traffic
Cautions
  • Whitelists bypass security rules
  • Can create security blind spots
  • May hide malicious activity
  • Require ongoing maintenance
Avoid
  • Whitelisting entire public domains
  • Adding entries without justification
  • Leaving old entries active
  • Ignoring whitelist impact
