Keyword Configuration Guide
Literal Keywords

Simple text matching that searches for exact phrases (case-insensitive).

Examples:
  • confidential - Matches any email containing "confidential"
  • social security - Matches "social security number", etc.
  • password - Matches emails with "password" in subject
Best for: Simple phrases, common sensitive terms, policy violations.
Regular Expressions

Advanced pattern matching for complex searches and data formats.

Examples:
  • \b\d{3}-\d{2}-\d{4}\b - SSN format (123-45-6789)
  • \b\d{4}[\s-]?\d{4}[\s-]?\d{4}[\s-]?\d{4}\b - Credit card numbers
  • (urgent|asap|immediate).{0,20}(action|response) - Urgent requests
Caution: Complex regex can impact performance. Test patterns carefully.
Common Use Cases
  • Detecting sensitive data (SSN, credit cards)
  • Policy violation keywords
  • Phishing indicators
  • Compliance monitoring
  • Threat intelligence terms
How Keywords Work
  • Searched in email subjects only
  • Case-insensitive matching
  • Increases ML risk score when matched
  • Can trigger specific rules
  • Logged for audit purposes
Performance Tips
  • Use specific terms over broad ones
  • Avoid very complex regex patterns
  • Test patterns on sample data
  • Monitor keyword hit rates
  • Remove unused keywords
Note: Keywords are used for risk scoring and rule matching. They help identify potentially sensitive or suspicious email content automatically.
